Chicken Parm Recipe:

How to make a smashing Chicken Parm:

Chicken Parmesan, also known as Chicken Parmigiana, is a mouthwatering Italian-American classic that brings together the savory flavors of breaded and fried chicken with the richness of tomato sauce and melted cheese. The dish starts with boneless, skinless chicken breasts, which are seasoned, coated in flour, dipped in beaten eggs, and then encrusted with a mixture of breadcrumbs and grated Parmesan cheese. The chicken is pan-fried to a golden perfection, creating a crispy exterior that contrasts wonderfully with the tender meat inside. Once cooked, the chicken is placed on a bed of marinara sauce, generously topped with shredded mozzarella cheese, and baked until the cheese becomes irresistibly gooey and bubbly. The result is a symphony of textures and flavors, where the crunch of the breadcrumb coating meets the lusciousness of melted cheese and the robustness of tomato sauce.

The history of Chicken Parmesan can be traced back to Italian immigrants in the United States, particularly in the vibrant culinary scene of New York City. While its exact origins are debated, Chicken Parmesan is believed to be an adaptation of the traditional Italian dish Eggplant Parmesan (Melanzane alla Parmigiana). The substitution of eggplant with chicken was a natural evolution, influenced by the availability and preference for poultry in American cuisine. Over time, Chicken Parmesan has become a beloved staple on Italian-American restaurant menus and home kitchens alike, cherished for its hearty and comforting appeal. Its popularity has transcended cultural boundaries, making it a go-to comfort food for many, celebrated for its satisfying combination of crispy chicken, tangy tomato sauce, and gooey melted cheese.

Follow along with the steps below to make your very own Chicken Parm!

    Ingredients:
  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)
    How to actually make Chicken Parm:
  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  3. Dredge each chicken breast in flour, shaking off any excess.
  4. Dip the chicken into the beaten eggs, ensuring it's well-coated.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the breadcrumbs and grated Parmesan cheese.
  6. Coat each chicken breast with the breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere the crumbs.
  7. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add a few tablespoons of olive oil.
  8. Cook the chicken breasts for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden brown and cooked through.
  9. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of marinara sauce.
  10. Place the cooked chicken breasts on top of the sauce.
  11. Spoon more marinara sauce over each chicken breast.
  12. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese over the chicken.
  13. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  14. Optionally, garnish with fresh basil or parsley before serving.
